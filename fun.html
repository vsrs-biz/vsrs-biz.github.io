Museums and galleries

If you haven't been, it's definitely worth visiting:
  • The Art Institute (open Monday and then Thursday-Sunday, 11a-5p). It's fantastic. Be sure to check out the new modern wing (an '09 Renzo Piano addition), and the unmatched collection of Cornell boxes. There will be two exhibits going on during the week of the wedding: Igshaan Adams (more info about the artist here) and Mel Bochner (more info about the artist here).
  • The Museum of Contemporary Art (closed Monday, open 10a-5p except Tuesday, which has extended hours until 9p). Yes, that's where the wedding is---and the galleries will be open during the wedding. Still, if you want more time to focus on the art, I encourage you to visit! The Nick Cave show (also open during the wedding) will be fantastic.
  • The Oriental Institute on the U Chicago campus (closed Monday, hours 10a-4p). Selection of Assyrian, Anatolian, Egyptian, Nubian, Megiddan, and Persian art. The re-creation of an Assyrian courtyard is notable.
Beyond art, there are other cool cultural institutions:
  • The Field Museum (natural history). Famous for its giant T-Rex, Sue.
  • The Museum of Science and Industry. If you want to learn more about how static electricity works or how chickens hatch, this is your spot.
  • The Adler Planetarium. Planetarium. Haven't been in 20+ years, but seems popular.
  • The Shedd Aquarium. Awesome aquarium that supports a lot of research and rescue operations. Many years ago, the Orcas learned to imitate the sound of a broken washing machine because they liked the hubbub of bringing in (confused) repair technicians.
  • The Brookfield Zoo.

Parks and nature

This is Fraser writing, so the nature info isn't top notch. But here's what I've managed to come up with:
  • The lake. Lake Michigan is very large and visible e.g., from Lakeshore Drive (often abbreviated LSD on roadwork signs). Check out The Point if you're on the South side, or one of the many beaches if you're further North. There's also a great running trail alongside the lake.
  • The dunes.
  • Millenium Park. Hard to miss if you're downtown, but it's worth walking around, taking a picture with The Bean ('03), etc.
